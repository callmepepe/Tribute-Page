  • 1899 - Born in Cicero [now Oak Park], Illinois
  • 1917 - He graduated high school he did not enter college but went to Kansas City, where he was employed as a reporter for the Star.
  • 1918 - Not yet19 years old, he was injured on the Austro-Italian front at Fossalta di Piave. Decorated for heroism and hospitalized in Milan, he fell in love with a Red Cross nurse, Agnes von Kurowsky, who declined to marry him.
  • 1924 - "In Our Time" his first important book was published in Paris. It was published the next year in New York City.
  • 1926 - He published "The Sun Also Rises" his first successful novel. It deals with a group of aimless expatriates in France and Spain—members of the postwar Lost Generation.
  • 1929 - "Farewell to Arms" is published Hemingway developed a grim but lyrical novel of great power, fusing love story with a war story.
  • 1932 - "Death in the Afternoon" a result of his passion for bullfighting a learned study of a spectacle he saw more as tragic ceremony than as sport.
  • 1933-34 - He took in the big-game region of Tanganyika
  • 1940 - "For Whom the Bell Tolls" the harvest of Hemingway’s considerable experience of Spain in war and peace
  • 1944 - Attaching himself to the 22nd Regiment of the 4th Infantry Division, he saw a good deal of action in Normandy and in the Battle of the Bulge.
  • 1953 - He received the Pulitzer Prize in fiction for "The Old Man and the Sea."
  • 1954 - Won the Nobel Prize for Literature. He was noted both for the intense masculinity of his writing and for his adventurous and widely publicized life.
  • 1060 - Left Cuba to for Ketchum, Idaho.
  • 1961- Died (suicide) in Ketchum, Idaho
